Page 03 — The Stakes

Risks & Consequences

The flood of AI-generated content is more than just an inconvenience. Here's the real harm it's causing and why some experts call it one of the biggest threats to society today.

Why does fake content online matter so much?

Imagine you're trying to make a decision, who to vote for, whether a health claim is true, whether a business is trustworthy. You turn to the internet to find information. Now imagine that a large portion of that information was generated by machines designed to sound credible, not to be credible.

That's the environment we're heading toward. And the consequences aren't abstract. They show up in elections, public health, financial markets, personal relationships, and more.

Global Warning The World Economic Forum has ranked AI-amplified misinformation and disinformation among the world's top global risks, putting it alongside climate change and economic instability on its list of threats to civilization. (Source: Naffi / UNESCO, 2025)

Photo: AbsolutVision / Unsplash — When fake content looks like real news, trust in all information erodes.

The five biggest consequences

🏛

1. Political Misinformation and Election Interference

AI makes it easy to generate fake quotes from politicians, fabricated news articles, and deepfake videos that look like real speeches. These can be mass-produced and distributed across social media in minutes, far faster than fact-checkers can respond.

During election season, synthetic content can sow confusion, suppress voter turnout, damage candidates unfairly, or make people believe events happened that never did. Unlike old-fashioned propaganda, AI-powered disinformation is cheap to produce, hard to trace, and nearly impossible to stop once it spreads.

High Severity
📰

2. The Collapse of Trust in Journalism

When fake articles look identical to real ones, readers stop trusting any articles. This is a serious problem for legitimate journalism, which serves a vital function in a democratic society.

If people cannot reliably distinguish a fake news story from a vetted, fact-checked report, they may stop trusting news sources entirely, including the good ones. The result is an information vacuum that misinformation is all too happy to fill.

Medium-High Severity
🧠

3. The "Dead Internet" Effect

Some researchers use the term "dead internet" to describe what happens when AI-generated content overwhelms the web to the point where human-authored material becomes nearly impossible to find. It's not that the internet disappears, it's that it becomes essentially useless as a source of genuine information.

As one expert put it: "The internet would be there just for entertainment. If this invasion of AI-generated content is not contained, the internet will turn itself into a dead internet. It will be there, but its credibility will be gone."

High Severity
💊

4. Public Health Risks

AI-generated health misinformation is already a documented problem. Synthetic "expert" voices, fake research summaries, and deepfake doctors can promote harmful treatments, discourage vaccination, or create panic about non-existent diseases.

During public health crises, when accurate information is literally a matter of life and death, the ability of synthetic content to overwhelm real medical guidance is a genuine danger to communities.

High Severity
👤

5. Personal Harm and Identity-Based Abuse

Deepfake technology is increasingly used to target individuals, creating fake intimate images of real people, fabricating videos of someone committing a crime, or cloning someone's voice to scam their family members. Victims of these attacks often have little legal recourse.

Voice-cloning scams alone, where criminals clone a loved one's voice to beg for emergency money, have surged dramatically, with millions of dollars lost by ordinary people who simply couldn't tell the difference.

High Severity

How misinformation spreads: the pipeline

Fake content follows a predictable path that amplifies its impact far beyond its point of origin. Understanding this process is the first step to fighting it.
