What Is AI-Generated Content?

Before we can understand the problem, we need to understand what we're actually dealing with, and why it's so hard to spot.

What does "AI-generated" actually mean?

Think about a factory that can produce millions of articles, photos, and videos automatically, with no human worker in sight. That's essentially what modern AI tools do, except instead of making cars or boxes, they make content.

When we say "AI-generated content," we mean any text, image, audio clip, or video that was created by an artificial intelligence program rather than by a human. A person might type a simple prompt, like "write a news article about the economy", and within seconds, the AI spits out a polished, convincing piece of writing.

You've likely heard of tools like ChatGPT (for text), Midjourney or DALL-E (for images), and ElevenLabs (for voice). These are the engines driving the explosion of synthetic content online.

Key Term: Synthetic Content "Synthetic content" is just another way of saying AI-generated content. It's called "synthetic" because it's artificially made to imitate something real, the way synthetic fabric imitates natural cotton.

Why is it so hard to tell fake from real?

Here's a helpful way to think about it: imagine a masterful forger who can copy any famous painting so precisely that even museum curators can't tell the difference. Now imagine millions of those forgers working simultaneously, flooding the art market with copies.

That's the situation we're in with digital content. AI models learn by studying billions of real human-made texts and images. They then reproduce the style, tone, structure, and even the small imperfections of real content. The result is material that feels genuine because it was modeled directly on genuine things.

The Shocking Number A 2025 study found that only 0.1% of people, that's 1 in 1,000, could correctly identify all fake and real media in a test. The rest were fooled at least once. (Source: iProov / Keepnet Labs, 2025)

What do experts mean by "deepfakes"?

A deepfake is a type of AI-generated media, usually video or audio, that realistically depicts a real person doing or saying something they never actually did or said. The word "deep" comes from the type of AI technology used (called "deep learning"), and "fake" describes what it produces.

You might imagine this as a digital puppet show, where AI pulls the strings of a real person's face and voice to perform a script they never agreed to. Once uploaded online, these videos can spread instantly, and corrections rarely catch up to the original lie.

"People can no longer confidently differentiate what's true and what's not. When that happens, they become skeptical of everything, even the most accurate, evidence-based information."

Why did this suddenly become a big problem?

For most of the internet's history, creating convincing fake media required real skill, expensive software, and significant time. A fake image might take a professional video editor hours or days to produce. That friction kept the volume of synthetic content low.

Then, in late 2022, generative AI tools became publicly available. Suddenly, anyone, no technical background required, could type a few words and receive a polished, realistic piece of content in seconds. The cost dropped from hours of work to zero. The volume exploded.

Before 2022: Low Volume

  • Creating convincing fakes required technical expertise
  • Expensive software or skilled professionals needed
  • Hours or days to produce one piece
  • Relatively rare, mostly limited to sophisticated actors

After 2022: Flood

  • Anyone can generate convincing content in seconds
  • Free or low-cost tools available to the public
  • Millions of pieces produced per day
  • Spreading across all platforms, categories, and languages
