The Problem

You probably read something fake today, and didn't know it.

Generative AI, the technology behind ChatGPT, image generators, and voice cloners, has made it shockingly easy to produce realistic-looking text, photos, and videos. As a result, AI-made content is flooding the internet at a pace no one can keep up with.

This isn't just about spam or annoying clickbait. When fake content becomes indistinguishable from real, it erodes something fundamental. It erodes our ability to trust what we see and read online. And once that trust is gone, it's very hard to get back.

Why This Matters "If we leave this problem untreated, the increase in unverified AI-generated content will lead to erosion of public trust on the internet." — Tristan Gaudiaut, Data Journalist (2026)

The graph above tells a striking story. In early 2022, almost no articles online were made by AI. By late 2023, AI was producing nearly 4 out of every 10 articles published. Researchers now predict AI content could make up 90% of all online content by the end of 2026.
